Implementation Checklist

Step-by-step checklist to guide you through the process of starting your mobile auto-repair business in Sydney.

Implementation Checklist

Your Progress

0 of 0 tasks completed 0%

1. Business Registration

Obtain Australian Business Number (ABN)
Essential first step for business registration. Apply online through the Australian Business Register.
Australian Business Register
Register Business Name
Required if operating under a name other than your personal name. Register through ASIC Connect.
ASIC Connect
Register for Goods and Services Tax (GST)
Required if annual turnover exceeds $75,000. Register through the ATO Business Portal.
ATO Business Portal
Choose Business Structure
Decide between sole trader, partnership, or company structure. Each has different tax and legal implications.
Business Structure Information
Open Business Bank Account
Set up a dedicated business bank account to separate personal and business finances.

2. Licensing and Certifications

Obtain Motor Vehicle Repairer Licence
Mandatory for self-employed motor vehicle repairers or repair business owners in NSW. Available in 1-year, 3-year, or 5-year terms.
Apply through Service NSW
Obtain Motor Vehicle Tradesperson Certificate
Mandatory for anyone working on a vehicle's mechanical operation or structure. Requires Certificate III in Light Vehicle Mechanical Technology or equivalent.
NSW Fair Trading
Apply for Mobile Workshop Operation Permit
May be required depending on local council regulations. Contact your specific local Sydney council.
Obtain Trade Waste Permit
Required for businesses generating automotive waste. Apply through Sydney Water or local water authority.
Sydney Water

3. Insurance Requirements

Secure Public Liability Insurance
Critical for all mobile auto repair businesses. Protects against third-party claims for property damage or personal injury. Recommended coverage: $10-20 million.
Obtain Tools and Equipment Insurance
Covers theft or damage to professional tools and equipment used in mobile operations.
Purchase Commercial Motor Insurance
Covers work vehicles used in the business. Comprehensive coverage recommended.
Secure Products Liability Insurance
Protects against claims arising from repair work or parts used. Often bundled with public liability insurance.
Arrange Workers' Compensation Insurance
Required if you have employees. Provides coverage for work-related injuries or illnesses.
State Insurance Regulatory Authority

4. Equipment and Vehicle Setup

Purchase and Outfit Mobile Workshop Vehicle
Select appropriate vehicle and customize interior for mobile repair operations. Consider van size, storage solutions, and workspace requirements.
Acquire Diagnostic Equipment
Purchase essential diagnostic tools including OBD scanners, multimeters, and specialized testing equipment.
Purchase Repair Tools and Equipment
Invest in quality mechanic tools, jacks, stands, and specialized automotive tools required for mobile repairs.
Establish Initial Parts Inventory
Stock commonly used parts, fluids, and consumables for efficient service delivery.
Obtain Safety Equipment
Purchase personal protective equipment (PPE), fire extinguishers, first aid kits, and spill containment materials.
Design and Apply Vehicle Branding
Create professional vehicle graphics with business name, logo, contact information, and services offered.

5. Environmental Compliance

Develop Waste Management Plan
Create procedures for handling, storing, and disposing of automotive waste in compliance with NSW EPA regulations.
NSW Environment Protection Authority
Purchase Waste Storage Containers
Obtain appropriate containers for different types of automotive waste, including oils, coolants, and other fluids.
Arrange Waste Disposal Services
Contract with licensed waste transporters and disposal facilities for automotive waste.
Prepare Spill Response Kits
Assemble spill containment and cleanup materials for managing fluid spills during mobile repairs.
Complete Environmental Compliance Training
Ensure all staff are trained in proper environmental procedures and waste handling.

6. Operational Setup

Develop Service Packages
Create structured service offerings including Essential Maintenance, Comprehensive Service, Fleet Maintenance, and Premium Vehicle packages.
Establish Pricing Structure
Set competitive pricing for services, parts, and labor. Research competitor rates in the Sydney market.
Implement Booking and Scheduling System
Set up online booking system with real-time availability and automated confirmations.
Set Up Payment Processing
Arrange mobile payment solutions including credit card processing, electronic invoicing, and digital receipts.
Establish Accounting System
Set up accounting software for tracking income, expenses, invoicing, and tax compliance.
Document Service Procedures
Create standardized procedures for common repairs and maintenance tasks to ensure consistency and quality.
Implement Customer Management System
Set up CRM system to track customer information, vehicle history, and service records.

7. Marketing and Customer Acquisition

Create Professional Website
Develop a mobile-friendly website with service information, online booking, and contact details.
Set Up Google Business Profile
Create and optimize Google Business Profile to appear in local search results and maps.
Google Business
Establish Social Media Presence
Create business profiles on Facebook, Instagram, and other relevant platforms. Develop content calendar.
Plan Local Advertising Strategy
Develop targeted advertising for Sydney neighborhoods in your service area. Consider local publications and community groups.
Implement Digital Marketing Campaign
Set up Google Ads and Facebook ads targeting local customers. Focus on specific service areas in Sydney.
Design and Print Business Cards
Create professional business cards with logo, contact information, and services offered.
Establish Customer Referral Program
Create incentives for existing customers to refer new business.
Develop Business Partnerships
Connect with complementary businesses such as car detailers, towing services, and auto parts suppliers.

8. Workplace Health and Safety

Develop WHS Policies and Procedures
Create comprehensive workplace safety policies in compliance with Work Health and Safety Act and Regulations.
SafeWork NSW
Conduct Risk Assessments
Identify and document potential hazards and risks associated with mobile repair operations.
Complete Safety Training
Ensure all staff receive appropriate safety training for automotive repair operations.
Obtain First Aid Certification
Complete first aid training and maintain properly stocked first aid kits in mobile workshop.
Establish Incident Reporting Procedures
Create system for reporting, documenting, and addressing workplace incidents and near misses.

9. Launch Preparation

Test All Operational Systems
Verify booking system, payment processing, equipment functionality, and service procedures.
Execute Launch Marketing Campaign
Implement promotional offers, announcements, and advertising for business launch.
Conduct Soft Launch
Offer services to a limited group of customers to test operations and gather feedback.
Implement Customer Feedback System
Set up process for collecting and responding to customer reviews and feedback.
Plan Grand Opening Promotion
Create special offers and announcements for official business launch.
Schedule 30-Day Review
Plan comprehensive review of operations, marketing, and finances after first month of business.
